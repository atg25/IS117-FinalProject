Native Flora

The Pinelands is home to thousands of species of unique plants creating rich biodiversity. These plants have a variety of traits ranging from the carnivorous pitcher plant (pictured left) to the towering fire-resistant pitch pines. This diversity creates rare environment and a popular environmental research area. Native Fauna

In addition to the various plants in the region, the Pinelands is home to thousands of native animals that are just as unique as the plants. Some of these animals include the intimidating Timber Rattlesnake, the humble Barred Owl, and the adorable Pine Barrens Tree Frog (pictured left). Sadly, many of these creatures are either endangered or threatened, but many organizations are working hard to counteract this. If you would like to support this cause visit the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection to learn more and donate.
