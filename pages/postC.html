Post Colonial History

Once Europeans arrived in the Pinelands they quickly recognized and capitalized on all the potential of the area. On this page we will cover 3 important sections of the post-colonial history of the Pinelands: historical events, national influence, continued traditions

Historical Events

Although the Pinelands was not officially recognized until 1978, plenty of notable events occurred before that point. As noted on our Native American history page, one of these events was the establishment of the nation's first Native American reservation, the Brotherton reservation. Other events include the domestication and farming of cranberries and blueberries in the mid-1800s and early 1900s, respectively, the construction of the Blue Comet train line, and lastly the official establishment of the Pinelands National Reserve. National Influence

Not only did the above historical events better the Pinelands, but they also influenced the country as a whole. The invention of domesticated blueberries added another crop to America's agriculture industry, progressive transportation like the Blue Comet and Route 9 led the country by example, and the iron rich soil provided materials for countless weapons, tools, and hardware. Continued Traditions

The Pinelands is still an exciting and active community which both carries on the traditions of the past while still leading ground-breaking innovations. The cranberry and blueberry industries are still booming with giants like Oceanspray farming in the small town of Chatsworth and Hammonton being the "blueberry capitol of the world". Transportation is still a big focus of the area with residents still pushing the envelope for faster and more efficient intrastate public transit.
