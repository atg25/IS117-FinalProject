Indian Mills

Arguably the oldest town in the Pinelands since it was originally the Brotherton Reservation and later renamed. Indian Mills is a quaint small farm town with lots of rich history whether it be Congressman John J Gardner's estate, Red Men's Hall, or the occasional arrowhead you may find in a field. Brigantine

Yes, the Pinelands even has beaches! Brigantine is a serine coastal town with a tight-knit community and is a dream getaway! Although it may lack the vast forests that are so familiar in other Pinelands towns, Brigantine still has lots of wildlife and even has a wildlife preserve on the north end of the island. If your looking somewhere with kind people, beautiful beaches, and stunning wildlife, look no further than the island of Brigantine. Chatsworth

If you want the true "Piney" experience, then Chatsworth is the place for you! Located in the heart of the Pinelands, Chatsworth is a secluded town with plenty of hiking trails, fishing spots, and cranberry bogs. Chatsworth is actually one of the largest producers of cranberries in the area, harvesting nearly 30 million pounds every year. Overall Chatsworth is a great place to get in touch with nature and see what the Pinelands is all about.
